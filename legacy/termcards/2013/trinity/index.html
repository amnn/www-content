Week 1

Facebook Pre-Hackathon Workshop

Tuesday 23rd April, 18:30
Department Of Computer Science - Lecture Theatre A

This event is a Workshop ahead of the First Annual London Hackathon that facebook will be hosting on 27th April. This is a 24hour event, starting at 5pm with free food, prizes and travel to london . In addition the winning team will be flown to Facebook HQ in California. For more details on the hackathon itself, visit this page: facebook.com/londonhackathon

Facebook London Pre-Hackathon Workshop

Come by our workshop to prepare yourself & your team for the London Hackathon! Feel free to come by for one or both of the workshops. We'll have some of our Facebook engineers on hand to help answer all of your questions and set you up for success. Bring your computer & your teammates- these will be interactive sessions!

6:30-7:30pm

Location: Oxford University - Department of Computer Science - Lecture Theatre A
Workshop 1: Building a Website: In this session we'll go over the basics of building a website: html, javascript, php, apache, & more.

7:30-8:30pm

Location: Oxford University - Department of Computer Science - Lecture Theatre A
Workshop 2: Building on the Facebook Platform: In this session we'll go over how to build on the Facebook platform & teach you how to build social.

Make sure to register for the Hackathon! RSVP: facebook.com/londonhackathon

Week 3

Games Night

Wednesday 8th May
Department Of Computer Science - Undergraduate Social Area

This event is sponsored by Rebellion Games , who are providing us with some games to play for free! As always, pizza and refreshments will be provided.

If you are having trouble getting into the building on the night, call 01865 283599.

If you plan on attending you will need to bring a laptop with any games you want to play installed and updated !

You will also need a LAN cable and probably a power supply for your laptop.

Team Fortess 2 and Super Monday Night Combat are both free to install and play from store.steampowered.com/ (you have to install Steam first, though). It is also possible to install TF2 from another computer with it already installed on, we will probably be able to facilitate this but should stress that it is a much better idea to make sure it is installed and up to date beforehand! If there are any other games you'd like to play (and think others will have) bring them along and we might play them too!

Week 4

Capture the Pi Challenge - Workshop with Dionach

Wednesday 15th May
Department Of Computer Science - Lecture Theatre A

This week we have a guest company, Dionach , coming in to do an interactive session with attendees. You will need to bring your laptops with you for this event, and don't forget power cables. People will also be grouped up into teams.

Teams will be challenged to exploit vulnerabilities that have been setup on Raspberry Pis, in order to understand various types of software vulnerabilities.

Dionach are an information security consultancy based in Oxfordshire that specialize in penetration testing.

Week 5

GreenQloud Talk

Wednesday 22nd May
Department Of Computer Science - Lecture Theatre A

This week we have a guest company, GreenQloud , coming in to do a talk on Cloud Computing.

Tryggvi Larusson, CTO and Cofounder of GreenQloud will be joined by Jonatan Nilsson, GreenQloud developer and QloudPay Lead, to discuss the components, software, systems and security of the Public Cloud. They'll review the unique approach to GreenQloud's cloud structure from its renewable energy powered services to its architecture and APIs.

GreenQloud are a hosting company based in Iceland. They have data centres that utilise 100% renewable, carbon neutral energy.
