Those present included:

  • John Swinbank (JS), Secretary
  • Ian Lynagh (IL), Treasurer

Officers' reports

President's report

DF said that this term had been 'ok'. There has been an unusually large number of speakers pulling out or rearranging their talks. It has also been difficult to ensure we have venues for all the events due to the fire strikes.

Secretary's report

JS said we had gained ten new members this term, which is on the disappointing side of average. He had nothing else to report.

Emergency Spare's Report

GS reported that the cost of the t-shirts can be split into two parts: a set-up cost and a per-shirt cost. The set-up cost has been split between the current orders: it has been completely paid for. There is none left for future shirt sales to cover. Future committees should make t-shirts regularly to ensure that set-up costs do not recur. GS promised to keep good records of the t-shirt ordering process for his successor.

System Administration Co-ordinator's report

DH reported that all of our new members have been given network accounts. In order to encourage them to make use of this facility, we are planning a hands-on introduction to the network next term.

The Institute of Molecular Medicine has given us a machine. This has a RAID array and should be very useful, although it's not exactly clear what for yet! It's a dual Pentium Pro with 50 GB SCSI RAID and 512 MB of RAM.

DH was pleased to report no breakages this term!

Discussion: Compsoc Organisation

GS opened this discussion by explaining our problem. Currently, it's expected that most of our committee members are in their second to third years; they should have more time, as there have been no exams at the end of the second year. However, as more and more subjects are introducing second year exams, this model is broken. The idea is to encourage more people to join a bigger committee and hence lighten the load. GS said the idea of this discussion was not to force a future change in organisation, but that we should be flexible and open for discussion.

There were various suggestions of possible strategies. GS suggested that freshers should be encouraged to join the committee, but not to do much in Trinity term. Amanda Kerr-Munslow suggested that the committee should last for four terms, with two committees overlapping in Trinity term. GS said that future committees should be encouraged to co-opt people; this should not require constitutional changes.

GS pointed out that there is a mailing list for all those interested in Compsoc organisation; please ask to join @F { compsoc@ } if you would like to take part.
