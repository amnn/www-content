Annual General Meeting, Hilary 2003

The meeting was held on 4th March 2003. It was opened at 19:10 in Magdalen College.

Those present included:

  • David Ford (TF) (President)
  • John Swinbank (JS) (Secretary)
  • Ian Lynagh (IL) (Treasurer)
  • Ganesh Sittampalam (GS) (Emergency Spare)
  • Dominic Hargreaves (DH) (SAC)

1. Apologies

No apologies were received.

2. Minutes of Last Meeting

Although the contents of the minutes were accepted, the pedants had a wonderful time finding all the trivial mistakes.

3. Matters Arising

GS promised that recors of the t-shirt production process would be available soon.

The new Compsoc logo is currently being stored in GS's inbox; it should be given an official home on a Compsoc filesystem somewhere.

4. Officers' Reports

President's Report

DF said that this term had been 'ok'. Again. A lot of potential speakers for this term fell through at the last minute; mayb of them may be prepared to come next term instead.

Secretary's Report

JS said we had gained one new member this term, for a total of thirteen over the course of the year.

Treasurer's Report

IL reported a loss of £75 over the course of the year, taking into account £100 of sponsorship. We currently have £475 in total. Tristam Fenton-May demonstrated his mathematical prowess by pointing out that, at this rate, we will run out of money in six years.

Emergency Spare's Report

GS said that no more orders have been taken for t-shirts: anybody wanting one should hassle him next term.

System Administration Co-ordinator's Report

DH reported that we have gained two new system administrators: James Green and Alexander Frolkin. Furthermore, nothing broke this term.

Election of Officers

President

James Green was proposed by GS and seconded by DF. He was elected unanimously.

Secretary

William Whistler was proposed by Amanda Kerr-Munslow and seconded by James Green. He was elected unanimously.

Treasurer

Richard Devenport was proposed by William Whistler and seconded by James Green. He was elected unanimously.
