Annual General Meeting, Hilary 2002

The meeting was held on 19th February 2002, in the seminar room, Corpus Christi College. It was opened at 19:10, and declared quorate.

Those present included:

  • Tom Ford (TF) (President)
  • John Swinbank (JS) (Secretary)
  • Tristam Fenton-May (TFM) (Treasurer)
  • Ian Lynagh (IL) (SAC)

1. Apologies

TF announced that apologies had been received from Amanda Kerr-Munslow.

3. Officers' Reports

President's Report

TF said the society had enjoyed a successful year, with many good events. This term has been somewhat quieter, but saw the launch of our redesigned website.

He also announced the donation of a new computer for the society network, thanks to Simon Cole and AMD. He was pleased to inform the society that he had already dismantled the machine and found it well built!

He finished by thanking the other committee members for the role they have played over the last year.

Secretary's Report

JS said we had gained 17 new members over the course of the last year.

He expanded slightly on the topic of the new website, urging members not to use Netscape 4.x because of its horribly broken CSS support and many other bugs.

Treasurer's Report

TFM was pleased to announce that he had managed to produce accounts in time for the meeting. However, they contained a slight mistake as he had lost (and then found) a cheque.

Over the course of the year we seem to have made a slight profit, although payments for the black tie dinner have not yet been finalised.

System Administration Co-ordinator's Report

IL said that this had been a disastrous term, thanks to the death of Fishy on 22 January. This caused major disruption, both to the running of the network and to the sleep patterns of some of our valiant sysadmins. By the time of this meeting, however, pretty much everything was running smoothly again.

Election of Officers

President

Dan Kolb was proposed by TF and seconded by TFM. There were no other nominations.

Dan declined the opportunity to make a speech, but nevertheless was elected unopposed.

Secretary

David Ford was proposed by JS and seconded by TF. There were no other nominations.

David gave a brief introduction to himself: he's a first year mathematician, who joined this year and has exams next term.

David was elected unopposed.

Treasurer

IL was proposed by TFM and seconded by TF. There were no other nominations.

IL explained that, since he has finals next term, Ganesh Sittampalam has agreed to serve as acting treasurer for the next term. IL then hopes to continue as a postgraduate at Oxford, taking a full role in the committee.

IL was elected unopposed.

The committee would like to thank all those who have helped with the running of the Society in the past year. In particular, thanks to:

  • Ian Lynagh and the rest of the Sysadmin team, for the time and effort they have spent maintaining and developing the Compsoc network.
  • Ganesh Sittampalam, for playing a large part in the organisation of the black tie dinner.
  • Dan Kolb for booking us several rooms in Corpus.

Results

The committee accepted a friendly amendment to thank AMD for the new machine they have donated.

Any Other Business

There was no other business.
