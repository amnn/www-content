Extraordinary General Meeting, Hilary 2002

The meeting was held on 26th February 2002 in the lecture theatre of the computing laboratory. It was opened at 20:33, and declared quorate.

Constitutional Amendment

Text of Motion

The society's constitution shall be amended as follows:

  • Replace section 3.1 with "The Committee shall consist of between three and ten Members of the Society, who shall be responsible for the running of the Society."
  • Replace section 3.3 with "The Officers shall be elected from the Resident Ordinary Membership in Hilary Term, for a period of one year. Should an Officer be unable to continue, a replacement shall be elected at the next General Meeting, until which time the committee may temporarily appoint a replacement. Ex-Presidents of the Society may sit on the Committee (ex officio), in addition to the normal Committee Members, at the Committee's discretion."

Proposed: Tom Ford
Seconded: John Swinbank

Discussion

Tom Ford opened the debate. He explained that this emergency meeting was called as a direct result of the recent AGM. At this meeting, Ian Lynagh was elected treasurer, on the understanding that Ganesh Sittampalam would join the committee as acting treasurer until Ian had finished his exams. However, a careful reading of the constitution later raised doubts as to Ganesh's eligibility for a committee position. This emergency meeting was called to amend the constitution so that Ganesh's position was clear.

Tom acknowledged that this meeting had been called at rather short notice, and that amending the constitution in this way is an unprecedented step. However, he said the amendments were within the spirit of both the constitution and the wishes of the AGM.

It was generally agreed that, in making changes such as this to the constitution, we must be careful to preserve the emphasis of Compsoc as a student society.

Results

The motion was passed unanimously.

There being no other business, the meeting was closed at 20:45
